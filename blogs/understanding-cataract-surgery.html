What is Cataract Surgery?

Cataract surgery is a safe and effective procedure to remove the clouded lens from your eye and replace it with an artificial intraocular lens (IOL). At Madhurama Netralaye in Bhandara, we specialize in advanced phacoemulsification surgery, which is the gold standard for cataract treatment worldwide.

Cataracts are a common age-related condition that affects millions of people across Maharashtra and India. In Bhandara district, we see many patients who have delayed treatment due to lack of awareness or fear of surgery. Our experienced team, led by Dr. Durgesh Pashine, ensures that you receive world-class care right here in your hometown.

Advanced Cataract Surgery Techniques at Madhurama Netralaye

Phacoemulsification Surgery

We use the latest phacoemulsification technology, which offers numerous advantages over traditional cataract surgery:

  • Micro-incision surgery: Only 2.2mm or smaller incision needed
  • Ultrasonic technology: Uses ultrasonic energy to break up the cataract gently
  • Sutureless procedure: No stitches required for faster healing
  • Premium intraocular lenses: Advanced IOLs for better vision quality
  • Same-day discharge: Most patients go home the same day

Types of Intraocular Lenses (IOLs) Available

Monofocal IOLs

Provide excellent distance vision with good value for money. Most suitable for patients who don't mind using reading glasses for close work.

Multifocal IOLs

Enable clear vision at multiple distances - near, intermediate, and far. Reduces dependency on glasses for most daily activities.

Toric IOLs

Specially designed for patients with astigmatism. These lenses correct both cataracts and astigmatism simultaneously, providing clearer vision.

Frequently Asked Questions About Cataract Surgery

Is cataract surgery painful?

No, cataract surgery at Madhurama Netralaye is completely painless. We use local anesthesia (eye drops) to numb the eye, and patients experience no discomfort during the procedure. Most patients report only mild irritation for a few hours after surgery, which is easily managed with prescribed eye drops.

How long does cataract surgery take?

The actual surgery takes only 15-20 minutes per eye using our advanced phacoemulsification technique. However, you'll be at the hospital for approximately 3-4 hours including pre-operative preparation, surgery, and post-operative observation. Most patients are discharged the same day.

When can I return to normal activities after cataract surgery?

Most patients can resume normal daily activities within 2-3 days. However, avoid heavy lifting (over 10 kg), swimming, and strenuous exercise for 2 weeks. You can watch TV, read, and use computers immediately after surgery. Detailed post-surgery instructions will be provided, and our team will guide you through the recovery process.

Are there any risks associated with cataract surgery?

Cataract surgery is one of the safest surgical procedures with a success rate of over 98%. Serious complications are extremely rare. Minor side effects like temporary blurred vision, mild irritation, or sensitivity to light may occur but resolve within a few days. Dr. Durgesh Pashine will discuss all potential risks during your consultation.

Can both eyes be operated on the same day?

Generally, we recommend operating on one eye at a time with about 1-2 weeks gap between surgeries. This allows the first eye to heal properly and ensures your safety. However, in special circumstances, same-day bilateral surgery may be considered. Dr. Durgesh Pashine will advise the best approach based on your individual condition.
